The data

The data for the assignment is from a dual-task self-paced reading time experiment of van Rij et al. (2013). In a self-paced reading time experiment, participants read sentences one word (group) at the time, and they press a button (i.e., space bar) to see the next word (group) to read. The idea is that increased processing difficulty results in longer reading times. The experiment of van Rij et al. was a dual-task experiment: to investigate the effect of working memory load on sentence processing, participants were presented with 3 or 6 digits (depending on the working memory load condition) that they had to remember while reading short stories using the self-paced reading paradigm. The trial sequence was as follows:

  1. 3 or 6 digits were presented (depending on the working memory block)
  2. Self-paced reading of a four-sentence story
  3. Answering comprehension question about story
  4. Recall memorized digits
  5. Feedback on digit recall

The original research question was about whether working memory load changes the subject preference in adults when reading subject pronouns (‘he’, ‘she’) after a topic shift. Generally, when a subject pronoun is ambiguous 1, listeners prefer the grammatical subject of the previous sentence as interpretation of the pronoun. We found that with increased working memory load, this subject bias decreased. Working memory load was manipulated by asking participants to remember 3 digits (low WM load condition) or 6 digits (high WM load condition) while reading short stories.

However, in the current assignment we would like to check two other questions:

  • Part I: We would like to check whether participants show a preference for the left or right answer when they are faced with a higher working memory load.

  • Part II: We would like to test whether working memory load and focus on the digit task affects the reading of the first word in the stories. The first word in the stories always introduced the first referent.
